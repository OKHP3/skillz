# BABOK v3 Elicitation Techniques Reference

## §10.25 Interviews

**Purpose:** Elicit information from a single subject matter expert or small group through structured dialogue.

**When to use:** Process owners, key performers, decision-makers. Best for exploring nuance, exceptions, and unstated constraints.

**Key rules:**
- Open-ended questions before closed questions
- Probe ambiguous answers with "tell me more" or "what happens when that doesn't work?"
- Record verbatim for business rules and exception descriptions
- Confirm understanding by reading back key points

**Session structure:**
1. Context-setting (5 min) — confirm process name and scope
2. Happy path walkthrough (15–20 min) — step-by-step narrative
3. Decision and exception probing (10–15 min) — edge cases and failures
4. Business rules and constraints (10 min) — policies and governing rules
5. Systems and tools (5 min) — what they use and how
6. Open invitation (3 min) — "what else should I know?"

## §10.50 Workshops

**Purpose:** Elicit information from a group of stakeholders simultaneously.

**When to use:** When cross-functional agreement is needed; when individual interviews reveal conflicting accounts; when time is limited.

**Key rules:**
- Assign a scribe separate from the facilitator
- Use a structured agenda with time boxes
- Park off-topic items visibly (parking lot)
- Confirm all agreements before closing

---

## §10.14 Document Analysis

**Purpose:** Extract information from existing documents — SOPs, policies, system screenshots, training materials.

**When to use:** When existing documentation exists but may be out of date; as background before interviews.

**Key rules:**
- Cross-reference document content against what practitioners actually do
- Flag contradictions between document and described practice
- Do not assume documented procedures are followed in practice

---

## §4 Elicitation and Collaboration — Core Principles

1. **Prepare** — understand the process context before eliciting
2. **Conduct** — use techniques appropriate to the knowledge source
3. **Confirm** — validate understanding with the source before proceeding
4. **Communicate** — share findings with all affected stakeholders
5. **Manage** — track open items and resolution status

---

## Technique Selection Guide

| Information needed | Recommended technique |
|---|---|
| Step-by-step happy path | Interview (§10.25) |
| Cross-functional handoffs | Workshop (§10.50) |
| Business rules and policies | Document analysis (§10.14) + Interview |
| Exceptions and failure modes | Interview with historical incident probe |
| System interactions | Observation (§10.30) or screen walkthrough |
| Stakeholder priorities | Survey (§10.49) or prioritisation workshop |
