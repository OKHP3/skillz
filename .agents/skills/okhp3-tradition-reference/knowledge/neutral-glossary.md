# Neutral Glossary -- Abrahamic Terms

A reference glossary of theological, textual, and structural terms used across the three in-scope Abrahamic traditions. Definitions are descriptive and non-normative -- they describe how terms function within each tradition, not which tradition's usage is correct.

Agents presenting ARE content should use these definitions as the baseline for any explanatory copy.

---

## Governing principle

No tradition's terminology is presented as more primary, original, or authoritative than another. Where a term is used differently across traditions, all usages are given equal space.

---

## Structural terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Abrahamic | Of or relating to the religious family that traces its origin to the patriarch Abraham (Hebrew: Avraham; Arabic: Ibrahim). Judaism, Christianity, and Islam are the three largest Abrahamic traditions by global population. |
| Canon | The authoritative set of texts recognized by a religious community as scripture. Differs by tradition and denomination. |
| Denomination | A named sub-group within a major religion, sharing distinct theological positions, governance structures, or liturgical practices. |
| Scripture | Sacred text recognized as authoritative within a tradition. The scope of scripture differs by tradition (see Canon). |
| Tradition | In ARE, a top-level category: Judaism, Christianity, or Islam. Used neutrally; carries no evaluative weight. |
| Pew Research Center | US-based nonpartisan research organization; source for US religious demographic data used to determine ARE scope. URL: https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/religious-landscape-study/ |

## Judaism

| Term | Hebrew | Definition |
|------|--------|------------|
| Tanakh | תנ"ך | The Hebrew Bible; acronym for Torah, Nevi'im (Prophets), Ketuvim (Writings). 24 books. |
| Torah | תורה | "Teaching" or "Law"; specifically the Five Books of Moses (Pentateuch). Also used broadly for all Jewish learning. |
| Nevi'im | נביאים | "Prophets"; the second division of the Tanakh. |
| Ketuvim | כתובים | "Writings"; the third division of the Tanakh. |
| Talmud | תלמוד | "Study"; the central text of rabbinic Judaism; comprises the Mishnah and Gemara. Two versions: Bavli (Babylonian) and Yerushalmi (Jerusalem). |
| Mishnah | משנה | "Repetition"; the first written codification of the Oral Torah, compiled ~200 CE by Rabbi Judah HaNasi. |
| Midrash | מדרש | "Inquiry" or "exposition"; a form of biblical exegesis; also refers to the collection of rabbinic homiletical texts. |
| Halakha | הלכה | "The way to walk"; Jewish law as derived from Torah and Talmud. |
| Shema | שמע | "Hear"; the opening word of Deuteronomy 6:4, the central Jewish declaration of faith: "Hear, O Israel, the LORD our God, the LORD is one." |
| Brit / Covenant | ברית | The binding agreement between God and the Jewish people, initiated with Abraham and formalized at Sinai. |
| Tikkun olam | תיקון עולם | "Repair of the world"; concept of human responsibility to improve the world through ethical action. |
| Tzedakah | צדקה | "Justice" or "righteousness"; charitable giving as a moral obligation (from the same root as tzedek, justice). |
| PaRDeS | פרד"ס | The four levels of Torah interpretation: Peshat (plain), Remez (allegorical), Derash (homiletic), Sod (mystical). |
| Teshuvah | תשובה | "Return"; repentance; turning back to God and to ethical behavior after wrongdoing. |

## Christianity

| Term | Language | Definition |
|------|----------|------------|
| New Testament | Greek/Latin | The 27 books of Christian scripture recording the life of Jesus and the early church; shared by all denominations. |
| Old Testament | Latin | The Christian term for the Hebrew scriptures; scope differs by denomination (Protestant: 39, Catholic: 73, Orthodox: 76-78). |
| Deuterocanonical | Greek/Latin | "Second canon"; books accepted by Catholic and Orthodox traditions but not by Protestant or Jewish canons (Tobit, Judith, 1-2 Maccabees, Wisdom, Sirach, Baruch). |
| Apocrypha | Greek | "Hidden"; term used by Protestants for the deuterocanonical books; Catholics and Orthodox prefer "deuterocanonical." |
| Septuagint (LXX) | Greek | Greek translation of the Hebrew scriptures, compiled ~3rd-1st c. BCE; canonical for Orthodox Christianity; contains books absent from the Hebrew Bible. |
| Gospel | Greek euangelion | "Good news"; the four accounts of Jesus's life (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John) in the New Testament. |
| Epistle | Greek | "Letter"; the letters of Paul and other apostles in the New Testament. |
| Apostolic succession | Greek/Latin | The doctrine that authority in the church is transmitted through an unbroken line of ordained bishops from the original apostles; central to Catholic, Orthodox, and Anglican ecclesiology. |
| Sola scriptura | Latin | "Scripture alone"; the Protestant principle that the Bible is the sole ultimate authority for Christian faith and practice. |
| Magisterium | Latin | The teaching authority of the Catholic Church exercised through the Pope and bishops in council. |
| Theosis | Greek | "Deification"; the Orthodox doctrine that the goal of Christian life is participation in the divine nature (2 Peter 1:4). |
| Eucharist | Greek eucharistia | "Thanksgiving"; the central sacrament of Christian worship -- the bread and wine of Communion; significance differs by denomination. |
| Metanoia | Greek | "Change of mind"; repentance; turning from sin toward God. Often translated "repentance" in English Bibles. |
| Logos | Greek | "Word" or "reason"; used in John 1:1 to describe Christ as the divine Word made flesh. |

## Islam

| Term | Arabic | Definition |
|------|--------|------------|
| Quran | قرآن | "The Recitation"; the primary scripture of Islam; understood as the direct word of God revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Jibril. 114 surahs, 6,236 ayat. |
| Surah | سورة | A chapter of the Quran. 114 total. |
| Ayah | آية | "Sign" or "verse"; a verse within a surah. |
| Hadith | حديث | "Account" or "report"; a recorded saying or action of the Prophet Muhammad, transmitted through chains of narrators (isnad). |
| Sunnah | سنة | "Custom" or "practice"; the way of the Prophet Muhammad as recorded in the Hadith; a major source of Islamic law alongside the Quran. |
| Tawhid | توحيد | "Unification"; the absolute oneness of God -- the central theological commitment of Islam. |
| Allah | الله | "The God"; the Arabic word for God used by Muslims, Arab Christians, and Arab Jews alike. Not a separate deity from the God of Abraham. |
| Ummah | أمة | "Community" or "nation"; the global community of Muslim believers. |
| Fiqh | فقه | "Understanding"; Islamic jurisprudence; the process of deriving legal rulings from the Quran, Sunnah, and secondary sources. |
| Tafsir | تفسير | "Explanation"; exegetical commentary on the Quran. |
| Tawbah | توبة | "Turning" or "returning"; repentance; turning back to God after sin. |
| Tawakkul | توكل | "Reliance" or "trust"; placing one's trust in God; surrendering outcomes to God's will. |
| Zakat | زكاة | "Purification" or "growth"; the obligatory annual alms tax; one of the Five Pillars. Typically 2.5% of savings above the nisab (minimum threshold). |
| Salat | صلاة | "Prayer"; the five daily ritual prayers; one of the Five Pillars. |
| Shahada | شهادة | "Testimony"; the declaration of faith -- "There is no god but God, and Muhammad is the Messenger of God" -- the first of the Five Pillars. |
| Hajj | حج | "Pilgrimage"; the annual pilgrimage to Mecca required of all Muslims who are physically and financially able; one of the Five Pillars. |
| Akhirah | آخرة | "The Hereafter"; the afterlife; includes the Day of Judgment, paradise (Jannah), and hell (Jahannam). |
| Salam | سلام | "Peace"; shares a root with Islam and aslama (to submit). Used as a greeting: "As-salamu alaykum" (peace be upon you). |
| Nubuwwah | نبوّة | "Prophethood"; the doctrine that God has sent prophets to guide humanity, from Adam to Muhammad (the "Seal of the Prophets"). |

|---------|---------|--------------|-------|

| Repentance | Teshuvah | Metanoia | Tawbah |
| Peace | Shalom (שלום) | Eirene (εἰρήνη, Greek) | Salam (سلام) |
| Sacred law | Halakha | Canon law / denominational polity | Sharia / Fiqh |
| Giving | Tzedakah | Almsgiving / tithing | Zakat |
| Sacred text | Tanakh + Talmud | Bible (OT + NT) | Quran + Hadith |
| Prayer | Tefillah (Shema, Amidah) | Lord's Prayer (Matt 6:9-13) | Al-Fatiha (Salat) |
