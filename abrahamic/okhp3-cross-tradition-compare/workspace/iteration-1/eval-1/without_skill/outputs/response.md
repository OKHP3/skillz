# Forgiveness in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam

## Judaism
**Scripture Reference:** Micah 7:18
**Passage:** "Who is a God like You, forgiving iniquity and remitting transgression; who does not maintain His anger forever, for He delights in grace."
**Note:** In Judaism, forgiveness involves both divine mercy and the human responsibility to seek reconciliation with those they have wronged, particularly emphasized during the High Holy Days.

## Christianity
**Scripture Reference:** Matthew 6:14
**Passage:** "For if you forgive other people when they sin against you, your heavenly Father will also forgive you."
**Note:** Christianity often emphasizes forgiveness as a central pillar of faith, linking the receipt of God's grace directly to the believer's willingness to forgive others.

## Islam
**Scripture Reference:** Surah An-Nur 24:22
**Passage:** "...and let them pardon and overlook. Would you not like that Allah should forgive you? And Allah is Forgiving and Merciful."
**Note:** Islam encourages forgiveness as a virtuous act that reflects God's own attributes of mercy, suggesting that pardoning others is a path toward earning divine forgiveness.

## Connecting Note
All three Abrahamic traditions present forgiveness not merely as a legal discharge of debt, but as a spiritual practice that mirrors the compassionate nature of the Divine. While they differ in ritual application, they shared a common ethical imperative: that human interpersonal forgiveness is deeply intertwined with one's relationship with God.
