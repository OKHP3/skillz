The proportional representation rule for the cross-tradition compare feature ensures that thematic comparisons across multiple religious or philosophical traditions maintain a balanced and equitable distribution of source material. When generating new theme entries beyond the initial 20 seeded ones, you should adhere to the following principles:

1. **Equitable Source Distribution**: For each theme, ensure that every major tradition included in the application (e.g., Islam, Christianity, Judaism, etc.) is represented with an approximately equal number of core texts or citations. If one tradition has three citations, the others should ideally have two or three as well.

2. **Diversity of Perspective**: Within each tradition, select passages that represent different facets of that tradition's historical or theological development, rather than relying solely on a single school of thought.

3. **Contextual Parity**: Ensure that the "depth" of the comparison is consistent. If you provide a direct scripture for one tradition, aim to provide direct scriptures (rather than later commentaries) for the others, unless the theme specifically calls for legal or secondary interpretations.

4. **Linguistic Sensitivity**: Use translations that are widely accepted and respected within each respective tradition to ensure that the proportional representation is not just quantitative, but also qualitative.

By following these rules, you maintain the integrity of the "Cross-Tradition" aspect of the feature, avoiding bias toward any single belief system and providing a comprehensive comparative view for the user.
