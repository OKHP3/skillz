# Christianity -- Evangelical Protestant

## Scope note

In scope per ARE criteria: Abrahamic lineage (YES) + 1% US threshold (YES, ~25% Pew 2023). Largest single denomination in the US. Includes Baptist, Pentecostal, non-denominational evangelical, and other born-again Protestant communities.

---

## Identity

Evangelical Protestantism is defined by four core characteristics (Bebbington Quadrilateral):
1. **Conversionism:** the necessity of personal spiritual transformation ("born again")
2. **Biblicism:** Bible as the supreme and sufficient authority (sola scriptura); most hold to biblical inerrancy or infallibility
3. **Crucicentrism:** centrality of Christ's atoning death on the cross
4. **Activism:** the imperative to evangelize and to live faith publicly

US population: ~25% (Pew Research Center Religious Landscape Study, 2023)

## Key distinctives

- Personal conversion experience as a defining marker of Christian identity
- Sola scriptura: Scripture alone as the final authority; no Magisterium or council binds the conscience
- Believer's baptism by immersion (most Baptist/Pentecostal communities); infant baptism in some evangelical Anglican and Lutheran communities
- Strong emphasis on personal Bible study and small group Scripture engagement
- Evangelical Covenant, Southern Baptist, Assemblies of God, nondenominational churches
