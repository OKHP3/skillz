## Identity

Mainline Protestantism refers to the historic Protestant denominations that dominated American religious life through the 20th century. They are broadly characterized by:
- Ecumenical engagement across denominational lines
- Critical-scholarly approach to biblical interpretation (historical-critical method)
- Social justice orientation -- racial equity, economic justice, environmental concerns
- Theological diversity within a common liturgical and institutional tradition

US population: ~16% (Pew Research Center Religious Landscape Study, 2023)

Key denominations: United Methodist Church, Presbyterian Church (USA), Episcopal Church, Evangelical Lutheran Church in America (ELCA), United Church of Christ (UCC), American Baptist Churches USA, Disciples of Christ

## Key distinctives

- **Wesleyan Quadrilateral** (Methodist): Scripture, Tradition, Reason, Experience as four co-equal sources of theological reflection
- **Westminster Standards** (Presbyterian/Reformed): Westminster Confession of Faith, Westminster Shorter and Larger Catechisms
- **Book of Common Prayer** (Episcopal/Anglican): shared liturgical text binding Anglican worship
- **Ordination of women and LGBTQ+ clergy:** most mainline denominations have moved toward full inclusion
- **Critical scholarship:** mainstream biblical scholarship is integrated into preaching and formation
- **Ecumenical councils:** active participation in the World Council of Churches and National Council of Churches
